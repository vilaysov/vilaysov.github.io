IMPROVING IDENTIFICATION OF SYSTEM-DIRECTED SPEECH UTTERANCES WITHOUT USING THE WAKE UP WORD

The activation of these virtual assistants typically requires the user to first say a wake-up word or phrase such as “Hey Siri” or “Ok Google”. This often becomes cumbersome, especially when continuing interactions with the system, as the user must repeatedly utter the wake-up word before or at the beginning of each turn of the same or new interactions. The classifier alleviates this constraint by automatically detecting whether utterances are directed to the system without the use of a wake-up word.

Used stack:

  • Speech Recognition
  • Wake-up word
  • ASR
  • Word embeddings

Novel speech coding model entirely in the frequency domain yielding transparent audio quality

Proposing a speech coder entirely in the spectral domain as an alternative to temporal speech coders at low bit rate (approx. 24 kbit/s). Demonstrated via MOS (Mean opinion score) that resultant speech synthesis at 24kbit/s has the same range quality of the international standard AMR-WR (G.722.2)
